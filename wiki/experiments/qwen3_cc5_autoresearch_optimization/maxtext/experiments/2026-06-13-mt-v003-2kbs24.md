# Qwen3-8B MaxText 2k bs24 probe (mt-v003)

Honesty probe above [mt-v002](2026-06-13-mt-v002-2k.md) (7,915 tok/s/chip @
bs12, HBM only 37% thanks to host-offload remat): does MaxText's 2k batch
curve keep climbing where ours flattened? `per_device_batch_size=24`
(49,152 tokens/chip), everything else identical.

## Hypothesis under test

Reference ladder. **Predicted**: small gain or flat (their collective floor
is already overlapped; amortization past 24k tokens/chip bought us only
+1.5%); OOM possible (host-offload traffic doubles). The result completes
the best-vs-best comparison against our certified 7,871.

## Setup

- Cluster charles-v6e, image as mt-v002 (cached). Workload
  `alekseyv-qwen3-cc5-maxtext-v003-b24`.
- Command = mt-v002's with `per_device_batch_size=24` and new
  `base_output_directory`/`run_name=mt-v003-2kbs24`.
- GCS root: `gs://tpu-pytorch-alekseyv-us-central2/autoresearch/qwen3_cc5/2026-06-13-qwen3-maxtext-v003-2kbs24/`

## Results

**Attempt 1 (bs24): compile-time OOM by 129 MiB** — `Used 31.37G of
31.25G`. Largest allocation: **bf16[24,2048,151936] = 13.91G** — the
logits matmul output (`logits_dense/dot_general`, fusion.470.remat3).
MaxText materializes **bf16 logits** (vs our plain-CE f32 pair — half the
per-token CE memory; why they fit bs12 where our plain CE died at bs6).
Their 2k ceiling is bs12–~bs20; runner projects bs20 ≈ 29.5G (fits).

**Attempt b (bs20)**: 20 steps clean (exit 0) — **6.207 s / 323.5
TFLOP/s/device / 6,601 tok/s/chip = −16.6% vs their bs12** (7,915). No OOM
(28.0 GB; host temp 39.4 GB — offload traffic doubled). Loss descent normal.

| MaxText @ 2k | step | tok/s/chip |
|---|---|---|
| bs12 (mt-v002) | 3.106 s | **7,915 — their optimum** |
| bs20 (this) | 6.207 s | 6,601 (−16.6%) |
| bs24 (attempt 1) | OOM (+129 MiB; bf16 logits 13.91 G) | — |

## Verdict

**refuted** (the probe's implicit hypothesis "MaxText gains from bs>12 at
2k") — their curve is past optimum at bs20: per-chip throughput drops 16.6%
as the host-offload DMA (decoder-layer-input + 4 projections × 2.5× larger
activations) stops hiding. **MaxText's 2k best = bs12 = 7,915 tok/s/chip**;
combined with mt-v002 this completes the best-vs-best:

| Stack (best @ 2k, charles-v6e) | tok/s/chip | MFU (our accounting) |
|---|---|---|
| MaxText (bs12, offload remat, bf16 logits) | **7,915** | ≈40.7% |
| **jax lane (bs5, certified v060)** | **7,871** | **40.5%** |

**Δ = −0.56% — a statistical tie.** See the
[stack-vs-stack analysis](../../../../analyses/2026-06-13-qwen3-2k-stack-vs-maxtext.md).

## Next hypotheses

- None on the MaxText ladder — bracketed (bs12 optimum; bs20 regresses; bs24 OOM). An intermediate bs16 could add ≲0.5% to their number; not material to the tie verdict.
- (jax lane, parked) bf16-logits plain CE — MaxText's memory trick (half our f32 CE cost; would reopen bs6–bs8 ≈ +1.5–3%) — logit quantization changes numerics ⇒ requires the v062-style same-data parity gate before any adoption.
