# Qwen3-8B MaxText v6e-8 reference baseline

User-directed: run MaxText on the architecturally-identical `qwen3-8b` config
(4096/32q/8kv/hd128/12288/36L/V151936, QK-norm, untied — byte-for-byte the
same architecture as our jax lane) with the proven llama3-8B v6e-8 recipe
tuning (which reproduced its README at 44.6% MFU on this cluster), and use it
as the reference ceiling for the jax lane (current frontier 33.6% MFU @
seq 8192).

## Hypothesis under test

**Hypothesis**: MaxText qwen3-8b with the llama3-recipe tuning (bs3/chip,
seq 8192, flash attention sa_block*=2048 fused-bwd, remat_policy=custom +
decoder-layer-input & 4×proj host offload, recipe XLA flag stack) lands
substantially above our jax lane's 33.6% MFU — quantifying the stack gap and
naming the levers to port.

**Mechanism**: Reference run, not an optimization. Tuning params verbatim
from `llama3_1_8b_8192_no_collective_matmul` (the recipe validated at 44.6%
MFU for llama3-8B on this exact cluster); model swapped to `qwen3-8b`.
Expected MFU in the 38–45% band (Qwen3's larger vocab head + QK-norm cost a
little vs llama3).

**Predicted signal**: TFLOP/s/device printed per step (MaxText's own MFU
accounting); profile (xplane) at steps 11–15; host-offload + SC-offload
activity visible in the trace.

**Falsification criterion**: n/a (baseline); a crash or < 33.6% MFU would
itself be a major finding (recipe non-transfer to Qwen3).

## Results

Attempt-b: 20 steps clean (exit 0), wall ~3 min, compile 13.25 s. Steady
state (steps 11–14 + 16–19, profiler steps excluded):

| Metric | MaxText qwen3-8b (this run) | llama3-8B reference (same recipe) |
|--------|------------------------------|-----------------------------------|
| Step time (median) | **3.535 s** | 3.477 s |
| TFLOP/s/device | **416.5** (→ 45.4% by MaxText accounting) | 409.4 (44.6%) |
| Tokens/s/chip | **6,953** | 7,069 |
| Loss | 12.43 → 2.29 (clean descent) | similar |

Effective tokens/step = 3 × 8 × 8192 = 196,608. Step-time spread across the
window: 7 ms — extremely tight.

**Cross-lane comparison (THE point of this run)** — identical architecture,
so tok/s/chip is the accounting-free metric:

| Stack | Config | tok/s/chip | MFU (our jax-lane accounting) |
|-------|--------|------------|-------------------------------|
| MaxText (this run) | bs3/seq8192, flash 2048 fused-bwd, remat custom + host-offload (layer-input + 4 proj), 24-flag stack | **6,953** | **≈ 39.6%** |
| jax lane (v019) | bs1/seq8192, splash, chunked CE f32-x, save_attn remat + skip5, vmem flag | 5,898 | 33.6% |
| Δ | | **MaxText +17.9%** | +6.0 pp |

Note on MFU accounting: MaxText prints 45.4% (416.5/918); at identical
tok/s-ratio our hand-counted formula yields ≈39.6% — their per-token FLOP
estimate is ~15% higher than ours (attention causal-factor / embedding
conventions). All wiki comparisons use tok/s/chip or our formula.

## Verdict

**supported** (reference baseline established). Two findings:

1. **The recipe transfers cleanly to Qwen3-8B** at llama3-parity throughput
   (−1.6% tok/s, +1.7% TFLOP/s — the vocab/QK-norm overhead is visible but
   small).
2. **SparseCore-offload flags are compile-fatal on v6e-8 under the current
   libtpu** (attempt 1: `HasSparseCoreLayout` RET_CHECK) — the April llama3
   run's jax0.6.1 stack tolerated them. Corroborates jax-lane v009.

## Observations — gap analysis (MaxText 6,953 vs jax 5,898 tok/s/chip)

> [!warning] Superseded by the measured [xplane bucket diff](../../../../analyses/2026-06-12-maxtext-vs-jax-bucket-diff.md) on 2026-06-12
> Point (a) below (batch as dominant driver) was refuted empirically:
> v025–v029 measured bs1→bs3 +4.9%, bs3→bs4 +1.8%, bs4→bs5 −0.5%. The
> measured driver is **exposed FSDP all-gather wait (74% of the gap)**;
> point (d) is also sharpened — splash is in fact *faster* than their
> flash path by 89 ms/step. Points (b) and (c) were confirmed (flags
> landed +10.3% as v026; accounting normalized throughout).

Original decomposition of the +17.9% (kept for the record):

- **(a) Batch: bs3 vs bs1 — the dominant driver.** MaxText fits bs3 at
  seq 8192 because its remat policy **host-offloads** the decoder-layer
  input + 4 projection outputs (zero HBM cost for saved activations) instead
  of keeping them in HBM. Our save_attn policy SAVES q/k/v/attn_out in HBM —
  that's why our bs2 attempt (v021) ran out of perf headroom and bs3 (v014)
  out of memory. Per our own v007 measurement, bs1→2 amortization alone was
  worth +40% at seq 2048; at seq 8192 the collective share is smaller but
  per-step fixed costs and remat interactions still favor bs3.
- **(b) Flag stack at the bs3 op-point**: the HOST_OFFLOAD scheduler bundle
  (which was flat at our bs1–2, v010) is load-bearing WITH actual host
  offload traffic to overlap — it ships as part of the mechanism, not a
  standalone knob.
- **(c) Accounting**: 45.4% (MaxText) vs 39.6% (ours) is pure FLOP-count
  convention — normalized out above.
- **(d) Attention kernel**: their flash-2048-fused-bwd ≈ our splash (same
  kernel family); v016/v022 showed block/knob deltas are noise-level here.
