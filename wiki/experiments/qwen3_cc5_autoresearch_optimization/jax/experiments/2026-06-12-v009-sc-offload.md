# Qwen3-8B jax v009 — SparseCore offload of FSDP collectives

Tests [qwen3-jax-sparsecore-offload](../../../../hypotheses/qwen3-jax-sparsecore-offload.md)
at the [v007 frontier](2026-06-12-v007-bs2-vmem.md) shape (bs2 + splash +
vmem flag): add the three SC collective-offload flags, nothing else.

## Hypothesis under test

**Hypothesis**: SC offload of AR/RS/AG lifts tok/s/chip ≥ 3% over the v007
frontier 6,095 by moving the 30%-of-step FSDP collectives off the TensorCore.

**Mechanism**: `LIBTPU_INIT_ARGS` adds
`--xla_tpu_enable_sparse_core_collective_offload_all_reduce=true
--xla_tpu_enable_sparse_core_collective_offload_reduce_scatter=true
--xla_tpu_enable_sparse_core_collective_offload_all_gather=true` on top of
the scoped-vmem flag. MaxText `ENABLE_SPARSECORE_OFFLOADING_*` bundle; llama3
v6e-8 precedent +3.4%.

**Predicted signal**: `all-gather`/`collective-permute` buckets shrink in the
op profile; SparseCore activity appears (SC tracks in the trace); step time
< 667 ms at identical shape; same HLO program shape otherwise.

**Falsification criterion**: tok/s/chip ≤ 6,095 + 1% noise → refuted
(inert-flag or SC-sync overhead).

## Setup

- Hardware: v6e-8, fsdp=8, tp=1, 1 slice of `<your-cluster>`.
- Image: `qwen3-8b-jax:latest` (trunk `3ef2b1e`).
- Workload: `alekseyv-qwen3-cc5-jax-v009-scoff`.
- Command (diff vs v007: the three SC offload flags):
  ```bash
  export LIBTPU_INIT_ARGS="--xla_tpu_scoped_vmem_limit_kib=81920 --xla_tpu_enable_sparse_core_collective_offload_all_reduce=true --xla_tpu_enable_sparse_core_collective_offload_reduce_scatter=true --xla_tpu_enable_sparse_core_collective_offload_all_gather=true"
  python -u train.py --batch_size=2 --seqlen=2048 --train_steps=20 \
      --use_splash=True --profile_dir=<gcs_root> --profile_start_step=12 --profile_steps=3
  ```

## Results

20 steps clean (exit 0); all three SC-offload flags accepted (no
deprecated/unknown warnings); loss trajectory normal.

| Metric | v007 frontier | This run (+SC offload) | Δ |
|--------|---------------|------------------------|---|
| Steady step time | 667 ms | **769.7 ms** | **+15.4% (regression)** |
| Throughput | 48,758 tok/s (6,095/chip) | 42,548 (5,319/chip) | **−12.7%** |
| MFU | 31.4% | 27.4% | −4.0 pp |

## Verdict

**refuted** at this op-point — −12.7% tok/s/chip, falsification criterion
fired. The llama3-jax +3.4% precedent (same hardware class) does NOT transfer
to seq2048/bs2 without the HOST_OFFLOAD scheduler bundle and the larger
seq8192/bs4-5 shape it was measured at. Knobs matrix updated; re-open only at
seq-8192 scale alongside the scheduler bundle.
