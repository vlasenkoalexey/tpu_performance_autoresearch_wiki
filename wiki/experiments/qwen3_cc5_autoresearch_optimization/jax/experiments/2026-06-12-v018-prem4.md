# Qwen3-8B jax v018 — partial remat skip-every-4 (HBM edge probe)

The last rung of the [v017](2026-06-12-v017-partial-remat.md) ladder:
skip-every-4 exempts 9 of 36 layers (one more than v017's 8).

## Hypothesis under test

**Hypothesis**: One more exempt layer (+0.43 GiB on v017's 30.68/31.25 GiB)
still fits and buys a further ~0.7% step time; if it OOMs, v017 is the remat
optimum at this shape (and the measured overage prices the vocab-sharded-CE
follow-up exactly).

**Mechanism**: `JAX_REMAT_SKIP_EVERY=4` on the trunk image (`b54d0c2`), all
else = v017.

**Predicted signal**: fits at ~31.1 GiB → step ~1380 ms; or compile OOM by
~0.3 GiB.

**Falsification criterion**: OOM, or tok/s ≤ 47,146 + 0.5% → refuted (v017
config final).

## Setup

- Hardware: v6e-8, fsdp=8, tp=1, 1 slice of `<your-cluster>`.
- Image: `qwen3-8b-jax:latest` (trunk `b54d0c2`).
- Workload: `alekseyv-qwen3-cc5-jax-v018-prem4`.
- Command: v017's with `JAX_REMAT_SKIP_EVERY=4`.

## Results

20 steps clean (exit 0); **no OOM** (the 0.57 GiB headroom held) — but
**slower**:

| Metric | v017 (skip=5) | This run (skip=4) | Δ |
|--------|----------------|-------------------|---|
| Steady step time | 1390.1 ms | ~1424 ms | **+2.4% (regression)** |
| Throughput | 47,146 tok/s | ~46,020 tok/s | −2.4% |

Loss smooth 12.10 → 12.07, no NaN.

## Verdict

**refuted** — the remat ladder is **non-monotonic near the HBM ceiling**:
one more resident layer (≈31.1/31.25 GiB) costs more in memory-pressure
effects (allocator/bandwidth) than the saved recompute. **skip-every-5
(v017) is the remat optimum at this shape**; ladder closed. This also
deprioritizes the freed-memory→more-skip rationale for vocab-sharded CE
(running hot against the ceiling regresses).

## Observations

- Optimal HBM operating point at this shape is ~30.7 GiB, not "as full as
  fits" — leave ~0.5+ GiB free.
