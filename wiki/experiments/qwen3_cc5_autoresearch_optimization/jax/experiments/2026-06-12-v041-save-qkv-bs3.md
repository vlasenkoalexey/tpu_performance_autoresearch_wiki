# Qwen3-8B jax v041 — save_qkv @ bs3 (+ bs2 cost check)

One-line remat-policy ablation motivated by [v039](2026-06-12-v039-bs3-saveattn.md)'s
612 MiB miss: `save_qkv` = `save_only_these_names("attn_q","attn_k",
"attn_v")` — attn_out (~6.75 GiB stacked at bs3) is recomputed in backward
instead of saved. CPU smoke: gradients **bit-identical** to save_attn.
(v041: v040 skipped — user's manual namespace.)

## Hypothesis under test

[qwen3-jax-save-qkv-ablation](../../../../hypotheses/qwen3-jax-save-qkv-ablation.md).

**Arms (both seq8192, scan + splash + chunked CE f32-x + 21-flag stack +
`--remat_policy=save_qkv`):**
- **A — bs2 cost check** (20 steps): vs v038's 2,633.6 ms / 6,221 — prices
  the attn_out recompute at the frontier shape (and is a fallback frontier
  candidate if recompute is free).
- **B — bs3 unlock** (20 steps, profiled 12–14, HLO): the payoff arm;
  PASS > 6,283 tok/s/chip; expected HBM ≈ 31.84 − 6.75 ≈ 25.1 GiB.

**Falsification criterion**: B OOMs, or both arms ≤ 6,283 → refuted.

## Results

Both arms clean (exit 0, no OOM, no NaN).

| Metric | v038 frontier (bs2, save_attn) | **Arm A: bs2 + save_qkv** | Arm B: bs3 + save_qkv |
|--------|--------------------------------|----------------------------|------------------------|
| Step (median) | 2,633.6 ms | **2,612.7 ms** | 4,413.5 ms (196,608 tok) |
| tok/s/chip | 6,221 | **6,271 (+0.8%)** | **5,568 (−10.5%)** |
| MFU | 35.7% | **36.0%** | 31.9% |
| Loss steps 0–2 | reference | **exact match** | descent sanity OK |
| vs PASS bar 6,283 | — | −0.2% (just under) | FAIL (−11.4%) |

- **Arm A**: dropping attn_out from the save list is FREE at bs2 — in fact
  +0.8% (20.9 ms/step), larger than the measured run-to-run band (~0.3%,
  v037-A↔v038): plausibly a real bandwidth saving from not writing the
  6.75-GiB-class attn_out residual. Under the +1% bar → no frontier
  declaration here; 50-step validation dispatched as
  [v043](2026-06-12-v043-bs2-svqkv-val.md).
- **Arm B**: bs3 now FITS (the v039 OOM is cured) but per-token throughput
  drops 12.6% vs bs2 — the q/k/v save mass at bs3 (≈10 GiB stacked) pushes
  the memory/compute balance past v6e's ridge; batch scaling on the
  save-list substrate is bandwidth-punished, mirroring (worse) the
  diminishing returns seen on the offload substrate.

## Verdict

**refuted** per the pre-registered criterion (arm B fits but −11.4%; arm A
+0.8% < the +1% bar). Two durable findings: (1) **attn_out never needed
saving** — the llama3-inherited 4-name list carries a free 6.75-GiB-class
saving; (2) **the batch axis is closed on the save-list substrate too**
(bs3 fits but is per-token slower — v6e bandwidth ridge). Arm A advances to
validation ([v043](2026-06-12-v043-bs2-svqkv-val.md)); if sustained,
save_qkv becomes the frontier recipe and the policy branch merges to trunk.
