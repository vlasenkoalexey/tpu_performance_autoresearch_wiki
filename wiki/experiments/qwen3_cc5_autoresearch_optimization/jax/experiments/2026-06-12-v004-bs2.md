# Qwen3-8B jax v004 — per-chip batch 1→2 (HBM cliff probe)

Follow-up to [v001-bs4](2026-06-12-v001-bs4.md) (refuted, compile OOM at
91.72 GB): locate the batch HBM cliff precisely on the baseline code. Tests the
still-open [qwen3-jax-batch-scaling](../../../../hypotheses/qwen3-jax-batch-scaling.md)
at bs2.

## Hypothesis under test

**Hypothesis**: bs2 either (a) fits and lifts tok/s/chip above the baseline
3,994 by amortizing per-step overhead, or (b) OOMs at compile, proving the
batch axis is fully blocked (cliff between bs1 and bs2) until the activation
transients are eliminated. Either outcome is decisive for the Knobs matrix.

**Mechanism**: Pure trainer flag `--batch_size=2` on the unmodified baseline
image. Linear extrapolation from v001 (fixed ≈16 GB + ≈19 GB/bs activations)
predicts ≈54 GB > 31.25 GB → OOM expected; XLA scheduling may beat the linear
estimate, hence the 8-minute probe.

**Predicted signal**: if it fits: collective share drops vs v000's 21.7%, MXU
rises from 19.9%. If OOM: compile-time RESOURCE_EXHAUSTED with the same
`[B,32,2048,2048]` + `[B,2048,151936]` dominant allocations at half v001's
sizes.

**Falsification criterion**: tok/s/chip ≤ 3,994 + noise (if it runs), or OOM
→ batch axis refuted pre-kernels (cliff at bs1).

## Setup

- Hardware: v6e-8, fsdp=8, tp=1, 1 slice of `alekseyv-tpu-v6e8-spot-xpk`.
- Image: `qwen3-8b-jax:latest` (unmodified baseline; flag-only diff `--batch_size=2`).
- Workload: `alekseyv-qwen3-cc5-jax-v004-bs2`.
- Command (diff vs baseline: `--batch_size=2`):
  ```bash
  python -u train.py --model_id=Qwen/Qwen3-8B --use_real_data=False \
    --batch_size=2 --seqlen=2048 --tp_parallelism=1 \
    --train_steps=20 --weights_dtype=bf16 \
    --profile_dir=gs://tpu-pytorch-alekseyv-us-central2/autoresearch/qwen3_cc5/2026-06-12-qwen3-jax-v004-bs2 \
    --profile_start_step=12 --profile_steps=3
  ```

## Results

Compile-time `RESOURCE_EXHAUSTED` (exit 1, 0 steps, 2.5 min wall):
**Used 43.12 GB of 31.25 GB hbm — exceeded by 11.87 GB.** The cliff is between
bs1 and bs2 on baseline code (linear ~54 GB estimate was high; measured 43.12).

Top allocations (compile-time buffer analysis):

| Size | Buffer | What |
|------|--------|------|
| 2.32 GB | `f32[2,2048,151936]` | fp32 logits at lm_head |
| 1.16 GB | `bf16[151936,4096]` | all-gathered lm_head weight |
| 1.16 GB | `bf16[2,2048,151936]` | backward logits gradient |
| ~20 × 512 MB | `bf16[2,32,2048,2048]` | rematerialized SDPA attention scores (≈10 GB) |

## Profile

Not captured — compile-time OOM before step 0; no trace possible (allowed

## Verdict

**refuted** — bs2 does not fit on baseline code; the batch axis on this
variant has its cliff at bs1. Combined with [v001-bs4](2026-06-12-v001-bs4.md)
(91.72 GB), the per-bs activation slope is ≈ 12–19 GB/bs against ≈ 15 GB
headroom. The vocab head (4.6 GB of the bs2 overage) and attention scores
(≈10 GB) dominate — splash ([v002, supported](2026-06-12-v002-splash.md))
removes the scores; a working streamed CE would remove the vocab-head
transients. Knobs matrix: batch scaling on this variant is **conditional on
kernel work**, not a free knob.
