# Qwen3-8B jax v016 — splash bkv=2048 at seq 8192

Tests [qwen3-jax-splash-bkv-8192](../../../../hypotheses/qwen3-jax-splash-bkv-8192.md)
at the [v015 target-shape frontier](2026-06-12-v015-save-attn-remat.md):
fwd kv block 1024 → 2048, nothing else.

## Hypothesis under test

**Hypothesis**: `SPLASH_BKV=2048 SPLASH_BKV_COMPUTE=2048` lowers the splash
share (23.7% of step at seq 8192) enough to beat 44,361 tok/s beyond noise.

**Mechanism**: env-only knobs in `splash_attn.py`; fewer kv-block iterations
per fwd kernel invocation at S=8192. llama3-jax precedent: bkv=2048 was the
seq-8192 MaxText-match (+0.7%).

**Predicted signal**: `splash_mha_fwd_residuals` share drops below 13.0%;
step < 1475.7 ms. VMEM within the 80 MB scoped limit (else compile-time
scoped-VMEM error like v005 — itself informative).

**Falsification criterion**: tok/s ≤ 44,361 + 0.5% noise → refuted (1024
optimal here).

## Setup

- Hardware: v6e-8, fsdp=8, tp=1, 1 slice of `<your-cluster>`.
- Image: `qwen3-8b-jax:latest` (trunk `a2f1914` — full stack).
- Workload: `alekseyv-qwen3-cc5-jax-v016-bkv2k`.
- Command (diff vs v015 ph2: the two SPLASH_BKV envs):
  ```bash
  export LIBTPU_INIT_ARGS="--xla_tpu_scoped_vmem_limit_kib=81920"
  export SPLASH_BKV=2048 SPLASH_BKV_COMPUTE=2048
  python -u train.py --batch_size=1 --seqlen=8192 --train_steps=20 \
      --use_splash=True --use_tokamax_ce=True --tokamax_ce_impl=chunked_xla \
      --remat_policy=save_attn \
      --profile_dir=<gcs_root> --profile_start_step=12 --profile_steps=3
  ```
