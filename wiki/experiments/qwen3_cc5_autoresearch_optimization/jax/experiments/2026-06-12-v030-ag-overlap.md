# Qwen3-8B jax v030 — AG overlap @ bs4/seq8192

Tests the [bucket-diff analysis](../../../../analyses/2026-06-12-maxtext-vs-jax-bucket-diff.md)'s
#1 lever: 624 ms/step of exposed FSDP all-gather wait at bs4 (74% of the
MaxText gap at the comparable bs3 shape).

## Hypothesis under test

[qwen3-jax-scan-ag-overlap](../../../../hypotheses/qwen3-jax-scan-ag-overlap.md).

**Phases (both bs4/seq8192, offload_attn + splash + chunked CE + 25-flag stack):**
- **A — scan + `--xla_tpu_enable_while_loop_double_buffering=true`** (26th
  flag): the XLA knob for pipelining loop buffers/collectives across while
  iterations. Launch-only.
- **B — unrolled (no `--use_scan`)**: the loop structure that exposed only
  ~4.5% collectives in v015. Re-tests v024's compile-OOM under the v026
  flag stack (HBM peak fell 7.7 GiB); compile-OOM is a valid result —
  capture allocations verbatim.

**Predicted signal**: arm A or B > 6,161 tok/s/chip (frontier + 2%);
async-done bucket shrinks materially in the profiled arm.

**Falsification criterion**: both arms ≤ 6,161 → refuted (AG exposure
structural; remaining path = manual weight prefetch, L effort).

## Setup

- Hardware: v6e-8, fsdp=8, tp=1, 1 slice of `alekseyv-tpu-v6e8-spot-xpk`.
- Image: `qwen3-8b-jax:v6e8-qwen3-8b-jax-20260612-v024-offload` (= latest).
- Workload: `alekseyv-qwen3-cc5-jax-v030-agov`.
- 20 steps/arm; arm A profiled 12–14 + HLO; arm B HLO dump (profile too if
  it runs).

## Results

Both arms crashed pre-step-0; wall 3 min 15 s.

- **Arm A (scan + double-buffering flag): never ran** —
  `ERROR: Unknown command line flag 'xla_tpu_enable_while_loop_double_buffering'`.
  The flag does not exist in this libtpu build (it is a MaxText-era /
  different-generation spelling). The hypothesis was NOT tested.
- **Arm B (unrolled + offload_attn @ bs4): compile-OOM** —
  `Used 67.67G of 31.25G hbm. Exceeded by 36.42G.` Top live buffers: ≥20 ×
  512 MB `f32[4,8192,32,128]` `checkpoint/rematted_computation/
  convert_element_type` temps — the identical unrolled-backward-liveness
  signature as [v024](2026-06-12-v024-offload-bs3.md) (59.41 G at bs3),
  scaled by batch. The new flag stack does NOT rescue the unrolled loop.

**Flag archaeology (local libtpu strings audit, post-crash)**: this XLA
generation renamed the loop-pipelining surface. Present in our build:
`xla_tpu_enable_collective_pipeliner`, `xla_tpu_enable_ici_ag_pipelining`,
`xla_tpu_max_ag_pipelining_per_loop`, `xla_tpu_enable_pipelined_loop_unrolling`,
`xla_tpu_collective_fusion_pipeliner_all_gather`, plus a
`WHILE_LOOP_UNROLLING_{DOUBLE_BUFFER,AUTO_UNROLL}` enum ("AUTO_UNROLL
unrolls by a factor of 2 if there is any collective present within a while
loop"). Retried with the correct spellings as
[v031](2026-06-12-v031-ag-pipeline-flags.md).

## Verdict

**inconclusive** — the AG-overlap hypothesis was not exercised (wrong flag
spelling for this libtpu generation). Secondary finding is conclusive:
**unrolled + offload_attn at bs4 is dead** (67.67 G, 2.17× capacity; v024's
liveness signature at +1 batch) — the unrolled fallback arm of
[qwen3-jax-scan-ag-overlap](../../../../hypotheses/qwen3-jax-scan-ag-overlap.md)
is closed; only the scan-side pipelining path remains.
