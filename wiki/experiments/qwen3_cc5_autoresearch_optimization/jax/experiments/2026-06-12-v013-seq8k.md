# Qwen3-8B jax v013 — seq 8192 probe (splash + chunked CE f32-x)

First attempt at the **program-target sequence length** with both validated
memory kernels on: splash ([v002](2026-06-12-v002-splash.md)) + chunked CE
f32-x ([v011](2026-06-12-v011-cxla-f32x.md)). Tests
[qwen3-jax-seq-8192](../../../../hypotheses/qwen3-jax-seq-8192.md).

## Hypothesis under test

**Hypothesis**: seq 8192 at bs1/chip fits in 31.25 GB with splash + streamed
CE (no S² scores, no `[BL,V]` logits) and trains at a measurable MFU —
or OOMs by a quantified margin that selective remat (v012) can cover.

**Mechanism**: Flag-only on the v011 image: `--seqlen=8192 --batch_size=1
--use_splash=True --use_tokamax_ce=True --tokamax_ce_impl=chunked_xla`.
Footprint estimate: 16 GiB fixed + ~14.8 GiB activations (2× v007's
8192-token/chip activation mass) + 2.49 GiB CE dw accumulator ≈ **33.5 GiB —
marginal**; the probe decides.

**Predicted signal**: either (a) runs — first seq-8192 datapoint on the lane,
MFU recorded; or (b) HBM OOM by ~2–4 GiB with activation buffers (not logits,
not scores) topping the list → v014 = + selective remat.

**Falsification criterion**: OOM (→ remat required; hypothesis stays open via
v014) or MFU < the seq-2048 frontier with no path to recover → noted against
the seq-8192 target.

## Setup

- Hardware: v6e-8, fsdp=8, tp=1, 1 slice of `alekseyv-tpu-v6e8-spot-xpk`.
- Image: `qwen3-8b-jax:v6e8-qwen3-8b-jax-20260612-v011-cxla-f32x` (reused).
- Workload: `alekseyv-qwen3-cc5-jax-v013-s8k`.
- Command:
  ```bash
  export LIBTPU_INIT_ARGS="--xla_tpu_scoped_vmem_limit_kib=81920"
  python -u train.py --batch_size=1 --seqlen=8192 --train_steps=20 \
      --use_splash=True --use_tokamax_ce=True --tokamax_ce_impl=chunked_xla \
      --profile_dir=<gcs_root> --profile_start_step=12 --profile_steps=3
  ```

## Results

Compile-time HBM `RESOURCE_EXHAUSTED` (exit 1, 0 steps): **44.88 GB needed vs
31.25 GB — over by 13.63 GB** (estimate was 33.5; the CE backward is pricier
than modeled). Both kernels confirmed engaged: splash config printed ×28;
chunked-CE autotune key `{"x":"f32[8192,4096]","w":"bf16[4096,151936]"}`.

Top allocations — **neither logits nor attention scores** (kernels did their
job); the walls are:

| Size | Buffer | What |
|------|--------|------|
| 2.34 GB | `f32[4096,153600]` broadcast | chunked-CE **backward** intermediate (v_block=2048 config) |
| 1.17 GB | `bf16[4096,153600]` pad | CE weight padded 151936→153600 |
| ~18 × 192 MB | `bf16[8192,12288]` | FFN backward `dot_general` intermediates (≈ 3.5+ GB live) + bulk of ~25 GB activation mass |

## Verdict

**refuted as configured** (seq 8192 bs1 without remat does not fit). The
hypothesis's contingency fires: activations ≈ 25 GB dominate the footprint;
selective remat's ~70% cut projects ≈ 27 GB total → fits with ~4 GB headroom.
The seq-8192 target stays open via [v014](2026-06-12-v014-s8k-remat.md)
(splash + CE-f32x + selective remat, combined image). Secondary lever if v014
is tight: smaller CE `v_block_size` (the 2.34 GB backward broadcast scales
with it).

## Observations

- chunked-CE backward materializes a `f32[H, V_padded]` broadcast intermediate
  (~2.34 GB) at its default autotuned blocks — the CE's real seq-8192 cost is
  backward-side, not the forward chunks.
