# Qwen3-8B jax v021 — bs2 @ seq 8192 on scan

The [retrospective](../../../../analyses/2026-06-12-qwen3_cc5-jax-retrospective.md)'s
#3 direction, unblocked by [v020](2026-06-12-v020-scan.md)'s −8 GiB live-set:
double the per-chip batch at the program-target sequence length.

## Hypothesis under test

**Hypothesis**: bs2 @ seq 8192 on the scan substrate fits (19.63 + ~8–9 GiB
activation/CE growth ≈ 28.7 GiB < 31.25) and lifts tok/s/chip above v019's
5,898 — the llama3-evidence batch lever at the target shape (their bs4 =
43.6% MFU).

**Mechanism**: Flag-only on trunk `11e8f9d`: `--use_scan=True --batch_size=2`
+ the seq-8192 recipe (splash + chunked CE f32-x + save_attn remat). 16,384
tokens/chip/step.

**Predicted signal**: fits ≈ 28–29 GiB; step time < 2 × 1470.9 ms (i.e.
< 2942 ms — amortization gain); tok/s/chip > 5,898 ⇒ **frontier shift**
toward the llama3 trajectory.

**Falsification criterion**: HBM OOM (projection wrong → measure overage), or
tok/s/chip ≤ 5,898 + 1% → refuted (batch axis stays closed at bs1@8192).

## Results

20 steps clean (exit 0); **no OOM** (the scan-substrate projection held);
no NaN; compile ~36 s/step (scan); loss smooth 12.10 → 12.06.

| Config | tok/s/chip | Step time | MFU |
|--------|------------|-----------|-----|
| v019: bs1, skip5, no-scan | **5,898** | 1389 ms | **33.6%** |
| v021: bs2, scan, uniform remat | 5,701 | 2873 ms | 32.7% |
| v020: bs1, scan, uniform remat | 5,569 | 1471 ms | 31.9% |

bs2 amortizes +2.4% over scan-bs1, and step time (2873 ms) beats the 2×-bs1
bar (2942 ms) — but the scan substrate's uniform remat costs ~5.6% vs
skip-every-5, which batch amortization does not recover.

## Verdict

**refuted** — tok/s/chip 5,701 ≤ the 5,898 bar. The batch axis at seq 8192
nets negative on the scan substrate because scan forfeits the skip-every
remat optimization (its 8 GiB memory win is real, but per-chip-batch
amortization at this shape is worth less than the remat-granularity loss).
**v019 (bs1, skip5, no-scan, 33.6% MFU) remains the frontier.** Scan stays
merged as the compile-time/memory tool; the batch axis at 8192 closes unless
a scan-body partial-save policy recovers the 5.6%.

## Observations

- Remat granularity > batch amortization at seq 8192 on v6e-8: skip5's
  −5.8% step beats bs2's +2.4% amortization. Any future scan-perf push must
  attack the scan body's recompute (richer save policy within ~10 GiB of
  freed headroom at bs2).
