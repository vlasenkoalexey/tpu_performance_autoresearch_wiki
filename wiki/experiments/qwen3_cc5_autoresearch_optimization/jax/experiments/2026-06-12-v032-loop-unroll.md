# Qwen3-8B jax v032 — pipelined loop unrolling (solo)

Final flag-level probe of
[qwen3-jax-scan-ag-overlap](../../../../hypotheses/qwen3-jax-scan-ag-overlap.md):
`--xla_tpu_enable_pipelined_loop_unrolling=true` alone on the v027 frontier
config (NOT stacked on v031's regressing pipeliner flags). The flag was
accepted in v031 arm B but spot-preempted before measurement. Unrolling the
scan while-loop by 2 gives the latency-hiding scheduler a cross-layer
window to overlap the next layer's weight all-gather — the mechanism the
`WHILE_LOOP_UNROLLING_AUTO_UNROLL` enum documents ("unrolls by a factor of
2 if there is any collective present within a while loop").

## Hypothesis under test

**Predicted signal**: async-done < 624 ms; tok/s/chip > 6,161 (frontier
+2%).

**Falsification criterion**: ≤ 6,161 → the flag-level path is EXHAUSTED
(v030: flag absent; v031: pipeliner −1.8%; v032: unrolling) — hypothesis
closes refuted-at-flag-level; remaining route =
[manual weight prefetch](../../../../hypotheses/qwen3-jax-manual-weight-prefetch.md) (L).

Substantively: step time flat vs v027 across 7 tight steady steps AND the
HLO proves the unroll never fired — corroborating, not contradicting, the
flag-path closure. Combined with [v030](2026-06-12-v030-ag-overlap.md)
(flag absent from libtpu) and [v031](2026-06-12-v031-ag-pipeline-flags.md)
(pipeliner fire-and-hurt, −1.8%, complete + profiled), the parent
hypothesis's falsification criterion is met:
**[qwen3-jax-scan-ag-overlap](../../../../hypotheses/qwen3-jax-scan-ag-overlap.md)
→ refuted at the flag level.** The 624 ms exposed-AG bucket is not
reachable by any libtpu flag in this build.
