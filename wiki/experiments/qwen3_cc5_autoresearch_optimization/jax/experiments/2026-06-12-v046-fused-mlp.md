# Qwen3-8B jax v046 — fused gate+up MLP

Inventory #2 (MaxText `fused_mlp` pattern): concat the gate/up `(out,in)`
kernels at apply time → ONE `[4096 → 24576]` matmul reading the hidden
input once → split. **HLO pre-filter PASSED** on the v044 dump: gate and up
are 10 separate `[2,8192,12288]` convolution kernels — XLA does not merge
dots over separate weight arrays. CPU smoke: loss bit-identical, grads ≤
1.3e-7 (f32 reassociation).

## Hypothesis under test

[qwen3-jax-fused-gate-up](../../../../hypotheses/qwen3-jax-fused-gate-up.md):
PASS > 6,849 tok/s/chip (frontier 6,715 + 2%; step < 2,389 ms). Targets the
52.2% conv bucket (halves FFN-input reads; fewer, larger MXU calls).

**Diff vs v045**: env `JAX_FUSED_MLP=1`. Everything else identical
(bs2/seq8192, save_qkv_ctx + context offload, 21-flag stack).

## Verdict

**refuted** — the manual fusion is performance-neutral. Interpretation:
the conv bucket's bandwidth is dominated by the WEIGHT bytes (identical
either way); the fused dot saves only the hidden-activation re-read, which
the baseline evidently already amortizes (and/or the concat pays it back).
The MaxText `fused_mlp` option is NOT a source of their remaining edge —
consistent with it being default-off in their configs.
