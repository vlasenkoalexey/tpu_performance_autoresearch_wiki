# Qwen3-8B jax v039 — bs3 + save_attn probe

The batch rung above the new [v038 frontier](2026-06-12-v038-bs2-saveattn-val.md):
HBM projects to the knife-edge (28.62 GiB at bs2 + the batch increment vs
31.25 cap). OOM is decisive data.

## Hypothesis under test

[qwen3-jax-bs3-saveattn](../../../../hypotheses/qwen3-jax-bs3-saveattn.md):
fits AND > 6,283 tok/s/chip (frontier +1%).

- Hardware: v6e-8, fsdp=8, tp=1, 1 slice of `<your-cluster>`.
- Image: digest-pinned `qwen3-8b-jax@sha256:bc8d7950…`.
- Workload: `alekseyv-qwen3-cc5-jax-v039-bs3sv`.
- Single arm: bs3 seq8192 + scan + save_attn + splash + chunked CE f32-x +
  21-flag stack; 20 steps, profiled 12–14, HLO dump.

## Results

**Compile OOM**: `RESOURCE_EXHAUSTED … Used 31.84G of 31.25G hbm. Exceeded
by 612.22M` (+1.96%). The v038 projection band was −0.27 to +0.48 GiB;
actual +0.60 GiB — knife-edge confirmed exceeded at the pessimistic edge.

| 6.75 GiB | `bf16[36,3,8192,4096]` | saved attn_out / hidden (d_model) |
| 2 × 1.69 GiB | `bf16[36,3,8,8192,128]` | saved K, V (8 kv-heads) |

## Verdict

**refuted** — bs3 + save_attn does not fit (wall measured: needs −612 MiB).
The batch rung on the unmodified save_attn substrate closes. However the
allocation table exposes an untested ablation: the save list's **attn_out**
(~6.75 GiB stacked) was inherited from the llama3 precedent and never
ablated — the backward already re-runs splash fwd, so dropping attn_out
from the save list may cost little recompute while freeing 6.75 GiB →
tested as [v041](2026-06-12-v041-save-qkv-bs3.md).
