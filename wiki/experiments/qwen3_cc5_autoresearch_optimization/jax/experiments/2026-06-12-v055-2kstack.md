# Qwen3-8B jax v055 ‚Äî 8k frontier stack at 2k

Third attempt at landing the 21-flag stack at 2k, on the only substrate that
both compiles fast and fits: **scan + `save_qkv_ctx_hbm` +
`SPLASH_RESIDUAL_CKPT_NAME=context`** ‚Äî i.e. the certified seq-8192 frontier
recipe (v048) minus io-layout, evaluated at the 2k op-point against
[v052](2026-06-12-v052-2kbase.md) (667.6 ms / 49,090 TPS / ‚âà31.6% MFU).

## Hypothesis under test

[qwen3-jax-2k-frontier-port](../../../../hypotheses/qwen3-jax-2k-frontier-port.md).

**Mechanism**: flags attack the 31.7% collective bucket (ring AG confirmed
active in v052's HLO); scan+remat is the carrier substrate
([unrolled+flags doesn't compile](../../../../observations/unrolled-flags-compile-blowup-2k.md);
[scan-no-remat doesn't fit](../../../../observations/scan-without-remat-oom.md)).
Context checkpointing keeps splash out of the remat re-run, minimizing the
recompute tax that made remat a +27% net cost in the old unrolled-2k era.

**Predicted signal**: HLO shows scan `while` loop + no ring CP-start chain +
`splash_mha_dkv_no_residuals` consuming saved context; peak HBM well under
v052's 29.81 GiB (8k analog: 22.03 GiB). Net step = (flag win on 31.7%
collectives) ‚àí (remat recompute tax at 2k). PASS = tok/s/chip ‚â• +3% vs v052
(step ‚â§ ~648 ms) + loss parity (12.0914/12.0986/12.0947 ¬±0.001). FAIL with
profile = decompose: if the collective bucket DID shrink but recompute ate
it, the unrolled+flags arm (120-min budget) becomes the clean follow-up; if
collectives did NOT shrink, the flags are shape-gated and the axis closes.

## Verdict

**refuted** (for this config at this op-point) ‚Äî the certified 8k stack is
‚àí2.1% at 2k/bs2; the PASS bar (+3%) is missed by 5 pp. Semantics preserved
(loss ¬±0.0001). Notable: the net remat+scan+flags cost is only +14 ms where
the old unrolled-2k remat measurements suggested +27% ‚Äî context checkpointing
has collapsed the remat tax. **The decomposition (did collectives shrink?)
and the peak-HBM reading determine whether this substrate still wins via
batch scaling** ‚Äî if HBM dropped to ~8k-analog levels (22 GiB ‚Üí much less at
2k), bs4+ opens, and batch amortization (bs1‚Üí2 was +40.5% at 2k) is the
remaining big lever.
