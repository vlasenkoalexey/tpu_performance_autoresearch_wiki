## Hypothesis under test

**Hypothesis**: Splash + remat reach the **program-target seq 8192** (bs=1) — the
sequence length the dense XLA-SDPA path can't reach (its `[B,H,8192,8192]` scores
= ~4 GiB/layer would OOM). MFU at the target seq should exceed the seq2048 frontier
(longer seq amortizes per-step overhead and raises MXU occupancy).

**Mechanism**: `--use_splash=True --use_remat=True --batch_size=1 --seqlen=8192` on
`qwen3-8b-jax:v006-splash`. Splash streams attention without materializing the
score matrix; remat bounds activation temp.

**Predicted signal**: no OOM at seq8192 (dense path would OOM); loss ~12 stable;
MFU at seq8192 > the 22.0% seq2048 frontier (this is the target-shape number).

**Falsification criterion**: OOM at seq8192, NaN loss, or MFU ≤ 22.0%.
