# Qwen3-8B jax v002 — batch bs=4

## Hypothesis under test

**Hypothesis**: Per-chip batch 4 (global 32) lifts MXU occupancy further than bs=2;
finds the occupancy sweet spot / HBM ceiling at seq2048.

**Mechanism**: Flag-only `--batch_size=4`; global_batch = 4 × fsdp(8) = 32 at
seq2048. Sweep partner to v001 (bs=2).

**Predicted signal**: higher MXU + tok/s/chip than bs=2, until the `[B,L,V]` logits
+ activations bind HBM.

**Falsification criterion**: tok/s/chip no better than bs=2, or OOM (→ marks the ceiling).

## Setup

- v6e-8 (fsdp=8, tp=1), BASE_IMAGE `qwen3-8b-jax:latest`, flag-only `--batch_size=4`.
- `python -u train.py --model_id=Qwen/Qwen3-8B --use_real_data=False --batch_size=4 --seqlen=2048 --tp_parallelism=1 --train_steps=20 --weights_dtype=bf16 --profile_dir=…/v002-batch-bs4 --profile_start_step=12 --profile_steps=3`
- global_batch 32, per-chip 4.

## Results

**OOM at compile time — never reached step 0.** Container exit 1, ~3.5 min wall (compile only).

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| HBM used | **91.72 G** / 31.25 G per chip — **2.9× over**, overflow 60.47 G |
| Failing op | `jit(train_step)/forward_backward … dot_general (bhqd,bhkd→bhqk)` |
| Dominant alloc | many `bf16[4,32,2048,2048]` (= 1.0 G each) **materialized attention-score** remat tensors (QK^T) |
| Steps / loss / MFU | n/a (never executed) |

The dense XLA-SDPA path materializes the full `[B, H, S, S]` score tensor; at
B=4, H=32, S=2048 that's the HBM wall. This **marks the batch ceiling for the
dense-attention path on v6e-8: bs≤3 at seq2048** (bs=4 OOMs by ~2.9×).

## Verdict

**invalid** — compile-time OOM, no run. Not a frontier regression; it's an
**HBM-ceiling marker**: the materialized-attention path caps batch at bs≤3 (seq2048)
on v6e-8. The lever to go past it is **splash attention** (v004 — kills the N²
score materialization), which also unlocks longer seq. Batch-scaling alone is
capped; the real headroom is the memory-win kernels.
