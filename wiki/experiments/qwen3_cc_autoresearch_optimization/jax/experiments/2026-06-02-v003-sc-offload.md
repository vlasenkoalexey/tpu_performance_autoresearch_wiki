# Qwen3-8B jax v003 — SparseCore collective offload

## Hypothesis under test

**Hypothesis**: Offloading the FSDP collectives to the SparseCore frees TC cycles
and lifts throughput at bs=1.

**Mechanism**: Flag-only `LIBTPU_INIT_ARGS` with the three
`xla_tpu_enable_sparse_core_collective_offload_{all_reduce,reduce_scatter,all_gather}=true`.

**Predicted signal**: `collective-permute` TC share drops; tok/s/chip rises.

**Falsification criterion**: no throughput change beyond ±2%, or a regression.

## Setup

- v6e-8 (fsdp=8, tp=1), BASE_IMAGE, bs=1/seq2048/bf16, 20 steps — same shape as
  the [baseline](2026-06-02-qwen3-jax-v6e8-baseline.md), so the flags are the only variable.
- `LIBTPU_INIT_ARGS='--xla_tpu_enable_sparse_core_collective_offload_all_reduce=true --xla_tpu_enable_sparse_core_collective_offload_reduce_scatter=true --xla_tpu_enable_sparse_core_collective_offload_all_gather=true'`. All three flags **accepted** by libtpu 0.0.38 (no Unknown-flag crash).

## Results

| Metric | This run | Baseline | Δ |
|--------|----------|----------|---|
| Throughput | 25,665 tok/s (3,208/chip) | 31,955 (3,994) | **−19.7%/chip** |
| MFU | **16.5%** (trainer) / 16.0% MXU (xprof) | 20.5% | **−4.0 pp** |
| Step time | ~638 ms | 512 ms | +25% |
| Loss | 12.09 → 12.08 (stable) | — | semantics OK |

Exit 0, clean run.

## Verdict

**refuted** — the SparseCore-offload flags are accepted and functionally offload
the FSDP collectives (visible as `async-done` 32.2%), but the result is a **−4.0 pp
MFU regression** (16.5% vs 20.5%) at this fsdp=8 / v6e-8 / bs=1 shape. Unlike the
llama3 jax lane (where SC-offload was +3.4% at bs=4/seq8192 with splash), here at
the small under-occupied baseline shape the offload's async overhead outweighs the
TC relief. **Do not use at this shape**; revisit only at a larger, splash-enabled
shape where collectives are a bigger absolute cost. Frontier stays at baseline 20.5%.
