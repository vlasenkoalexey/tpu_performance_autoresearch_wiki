# Qwen3-8B jax v019 — XLA flag stack @ seq8192 (transfer test)

## Hypothesis under test

**Hypothesis**: The MaxText HOST_OFFLOAD XLA flag stack that just lifted the seq2048
frontier +3.4 pp ([v018](2026-06-02-v018-xla-flag-stack.md), 35.8%) also lifts the
**seq8192** frontier ([v009](2026-06-02-v009-splash-s8k-vmem.md), 30.4% MFU, splash+remat
bs1). Same hypothesis (`qwen3-jax-xla-flag-stack`), different variant-regime — verifying the
win transfers to the program-target sequence length, where host-transfer/collective overlap
should matter *more* (bigger activations, longer collectives).

**Mechanism**: v009 config (`--use_remat --use_splash --batch_size=1 --seqlen=8192` + vmem
flag, image `qwen3-8b-jax:v006-splash`) + the 7-flag LIBTPU scheduler bundle + the
`xla_jf_spmd_threshold` XLA flag. Flag-only, no rebuild.

**Predicted signal**: MFU > 30.4% (target ≥ +3 pp like seq2048); loss parity (~12.07, no NaN); exit 0.

**Falsification criterion**: MFU ≤ 30.4% (the scheduler gains don't transfer to the
larger-seq regime), or a flag-induced crash.

## Setup

- **Hardware**: v6e-8 (fsdp=8), image `qwen3-8b-jax:v006-splash`. Flag-only.
- **Launch**: v009 shape (`--use_remat --use_splash --batch_size=1 --seqlen=8192` + vmem flag) + the 7 LIBTPU scheduler flags from the v018 bundle. global batch 8.
- **Flag note**: `--xla_jf_spmd_threshold_for_windowed_einsum_mib=1000000` (placed in `XLA_FLAGS`) was **rejected by this XLA build** — hard crash "Unknown flag in XLA_FLAGS" on first submission. Runner resubmitted without it. **All 8 LIBTPU_INIT_ARGS flags (scoped_vmem + 7 scheduler) were accepted.** This invalid flag was never actually in the v018 launch_cmd either, so it is not part of the real frontier config.

## Verdict

**Inconclusive** (within noise). The MaxText HOST_OFFLOAD scheduler flags that won +3.4 pp at seq2048/bs4 ([v018](2026-06-02-v018-xla-flag-stack.md)) are **neutral at seq8192/bs1**: +0.3 pp / +0.8%, step time unchanged at ~1,510 ms — indistinguishable from v009. The flags help when the step is compute/scheduling-bound with many concurrent ops (dense bs4 at short seq); at seq8192/bs1 the step is dominated by the long splash attention and FSDP collectives over big activations, where there's little scheduling slack to recover. **seq8192 frontier stays [v009](2026-06-02-v009-splash-s8k-vmem.md) (30.4%)** — 30.7% is not beyond noise. Useful negative: the scheduler bundle is a seq2048 lever, not universal. Also confirmed `xla_jf_spmd_threshold_for_windowed_einsum_mib` is invalid on this XLA build (do not use).
