# Qwen3-8B jax v017 — splash bs=4 + SparseCore offload (retry at scale)

## Hypothesis under test

**Hypothesis**: SparseCore collective-offload — which REGRESSED at the tiny bs=1
baseline ([v003](2026-06-02-v003-sc-offload.md), −4 pp) — may now **help** on the
v008 frontier shape (splash, bs=4): with a bigger, busier step the collective is a
larger absolute cost and offloading it off the TensorCore frees more matmul cycles
(this is the regime where it was +3.4% on the llama3 jax lane).

**Mechanism**: v008 frontier config + the three
`xla_tpu_enable_sparse_core_collective_offload_*` flags in `LIBTPU_INIT_ARGS`
(alongside the vmem flag). `--use_remat --use_splash --batch_size=4 --seqlen=2048`,
image `qwen3-8b-jax:v006-splash`. Flag-only.

**Predicted signal**: collective TC time-share drops; MFU > 32.4%.

**Falsification criterion**: no improvement / regression vs 32.4% (offload overhead
still > TC relief even at bs=4).

## Setup

- **Hardware**: v6e-8 (fsdp=8), image `qwen3-8b-jax:v006-splash`. Flag-only (no rebuild).
- **Launch** (diff from v008 frontier: `+` 3 sparsecore-offload flags in `LIBTPU_INIT_ARGS`):
  ```
  cd /app/trainer && LIBTPU_INIT_ARGS='--xla_tpu_scoped_vmem_limit_kib=98304 \
    --xla_tpu_enable_sparse_core_collective_offload_all_reduce=true \
    --xla_tpu_enable_sparse_core_collective_offload_reduce_scatter=true \
    --xla_tpu_enable_sparse_core_collective_offload_all_gather=true' \
  ... python -u train.py --model_id=Qwen/Qwen3-8B --use_real_data=False --seqlen=2048 \
    --tp_parallelism=1 --train_steps=20 --weights_dtype=bf16 \
    --use_remat --use_splash --batch_size=4 \
    --profile_dir=<gcs_root> --profile_start_step=12 --profile_steps=3
  ```
- global batch 32, seqlen 2048. All 3 offload flags **accepted by libtpu** (no rejection lines).

## Verdict

**Refuted.** Falsification criterion ("no improvement vs 32.4%") met: 31.7% < 32.4%. The three SparseCore collective-offload flags were *accepted and active* (HLO shows the offload ops) but the async-offload overhead still exceeds the TensorCore relief — even on the busy bs=4 frontier shape, not just the tiny bs=1 of [v003](2026-06-02-v003-sc-offload.md). This **closes the SparseCore-offload topic for the qwen3 jax lane**: it regressed at bs=1 (−4 pp) and at bs=4 (−0.7 pp). The llama3-jax +3.4% result does not transfer — likely because qwen3's collective share is already low (v008 profile: collective 6.3%), leaving little to offload. Loss stable, semantics intact.
