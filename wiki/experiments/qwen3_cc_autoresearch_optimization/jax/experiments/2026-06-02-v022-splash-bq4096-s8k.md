# Qwen3-8B jax v022 — splash larger forward blocks @ seq8192

## Hypothesis under test

**Hypothesis**: [v021](2026-06-02-v021-splash-bq1024.md) established that **larger `bq` is better** for
qwen3 splash (symmetric 1024 lost; `bq=2048`=seqlen won at seq2048). At **seq8192** the forward block
`bq=2048` is only ¼ of seqlen — growing it to `bq=4096, bkv=2048` should improve the attention kernel's
MXU efficiency and lift the seq8192 frontier above v009's 30.4% MFU.

**Mechanism**: v009 seq8192 shape (`--use_remat --use_splash --batch_size=1 --seqlen=8192` + scoped_vmem
+ the 7 LIBTPU scheduler flags) **+ env `SPLASH_BQ=4096 SPLASH_BKV=2048`** (upstream splash; dkv blocks
left at default 2048). Flag-only, no rebuild.

**Predicted signal**: MFU > 30.4% (target +1–3 pp); splash custom-call time-share drops; loss parity
(~12.07, no NaN); exit 0.

**Falsification criterion**: MFU ≤ 30.4% beyond noise, or a splash scoped-VMEM OOM from the larger
forward blocks (the v006/v007 failure mode — would mean retune down).

## Setup

- **Hardware**: v6e-8 (fsdp=8), image `qwen3-8b-jax:v006-splash`. Flag-only (env).
- **Launch**: v009 seq8192 shape (`--use_remat --use_splash --batch_size=1 --seqlen=8192` + scoped_vmem + 7 LIBTPU scheduler flags) **+ env `SPLASH_BQ=4096 SPLASH_BKV=2048`** (upstream splash). global batch 8.
- **Blocks confirmed** (all layers): `bq=4096 bkv=2048 bq_dkv=2048 bkv_dkv=2048 fused_bwd=True`. No VMEM/HBM OOM (scoped-vmem 98304 had headroom).

## Verdict

**Inconclusive** (within noise). Doubling the splash forward blocks (`bq=2048→4096, bkv=1024→2048`) at
seq8192 is neutral: +0.1 pp / +0.08%, no VMEM OOM. Combined with [v021](2026-06-02-v021-splash-bq1024.md)
(seq2048, smaller blocks regressed), **splash forward-block tuning is exhausted for qwen3-jax** — the
kernel's default-ish config (bq=2048 at seq2048, the maximal; ≈2048–4096 at seq8192, flat) is already at
its plateau; block size is not a lever here. seq8192 frontier stays v009 (30.4%). Block-tuning hypothesis
retired (neutral at both seqs).
