# Qwen3-8B jax v013 — tokamax CE canary (absl-guarded image)

## Hypothesis under test

**Hypothesis**: With the absl-flags guard, the mosaic_tpu streamed CE runs, is
numerically correct (loss ~12 stable), and is ~neutral on MFU at bs=2 (the canary
before stacking with splash).

**Mechanism**: `--use_remat --use_tokamax_ce --tokamax_ce_impl=mosaic_tpu --batch_size=2`,
image `qwen3-8b-jax:v013-ce` (absl pre-parse guard baked in). No splash.

**Predicted signal**: reaches steps; loss ~12 with variation; MFU ≈ 22.0%.

**Falsification criterion**: absl/tokamax crash, NaN/flat loss, or large MFU regression.

## Setup

- v6e-8 (fsdp=8, tp=1), image `qwen3-8b-jax:v013-ce`, `--use_remat --use_tokamax_ce --tokamax_ce_impl=mosaic_tpu --batch_size=2 --seqlen=2048`.
- global_batch 16. The mosaic_tpu CE **autotunes** on first call: step 0 and step 1 took ~135 s each (one-time; cached for subsequent CE runs), then steps settle to ~1003 ms.

## Results

| Metric | Value | vs remat-bs2 (v004, 22.0%) |
|--------|-------|-----------------------------|
| Loss | 12.098 → 12.085 (varies — **CE numerically correct**) | — |
| Throughput | 32,659 tok/s (**4,082/chip**) | 4,280 → −4.6% |
| MFU | **21.0%** | 22.0% → −1.0 pp |
| Steady step | ~1003 ms | — |
| Exit | 0 | — |

CE runs correctly but is **slightly slower than plain CE at bs=2** — expected: CE is
a **memory** lever (streams logits over V), and at bs=2 (which already fits) the
extra streamed-CE work isn't repaid. Its value is enabling **bigger batch** where the
`[B,L,V]` logits would otherwise bind HBM — that's the v014 (bs=6) test.

## Verdict

**supported (CE-correctness canary passed)** — the tokamax streamed CE (mosaic_tpu)
is numerically correct (stable, varying loss) and runs cleanly after the absl-flags
guard. It does **not** move the frontier at bs=2 (21.0% < 32.4%) — CE is a memory
lever, not a speed lever at a shape that fits. CE is now a **validated tool** for the
stacked frontier push (v014 bs=6) where it removes the logit-memory wall. The
qwen3-jax-tokamax-ce hypothesis (batch-unlock value) is settled by v014, not here.
