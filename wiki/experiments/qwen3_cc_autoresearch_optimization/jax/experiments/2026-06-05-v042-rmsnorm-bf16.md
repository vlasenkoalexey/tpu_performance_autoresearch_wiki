# Qwen3-8B jax v042 — RMSNorm bf16 weight-multiply @ seq8192 bs3

## Hypothesis under test

**Hypothesis**: our `Qwen3RMSNorm` keeps fp32 through the weight-scale multiply over `(B,T,D)`; MaxText downcasts
`(x·rsqrt)` to bf16 *before* the weight scale. Matching MaxText (bf16 weight-multiply) trims the fp32 slice of
the recomputed loop-fusion bucket → frontier > v035 (34.6% / 6,030).

**Mechanism**: image `qwen3-8b-jax:v042-rmsnorm-bf16` (FROM v033-maxtext-ce, ONLY the `Qwen3RMSNorm.__call__`
change: `y=(x32·rsqrt).astype(bf16); return y·weight_bf16`). v035 flags, pure FSDP bs3, NO tp, NO offload.
**fp32-identical** (the change collapses in fp32; equivalence test PASS, fwd Δ2.7e-7 / loss Δ0 / grads OK).

**Predicted signal**: tok/s/chip > 6,030 beyond ~±5ms noise. (Downgraded after the v035 profile showed SiLU —
already bf16 — dominates loop-fusion at 3,985ms; the RMSNorm fp32-multiply is a small slice.)

**Falsification criterion**: ≤ 6,030 within noise.

## Setup

- Image `qwen3-8b-jax:v042-rmsnorm-bf16`, `--use_remat --use_splash --use_scan --use_maxtext_ce --batch_size=3
  --seqlen=8192` + v035 LIBTPU bundle. Startup confirmed: `[mesh] fsdp=8 tp=1`, `global_batch=24
  per_chip_batch=3`, `[scan]/[attn]/[remat:nothing_saveable]/[ce]` ON.

## Verdict

**Inconclusive (parity)** — directionally positive (+0.16%) but **sub-noise**, so it does not clear the
"measurable beyond noise" bar for `supported`. Consistent with the v035 profile: the RMSNorm fp32 weight-multiply
is a small slice of loop-fusion (which is SiLU-dominated, and SiLU is already bf16). The change is still worth
keeping — it's fp32-identical, matches the MaxText reference exactly, and is directionally positive — but it
does not move the frontier. **seq8192 frontier stays v035 (34.6% / 6,030).** Could graduate to trunk as a
no-cost reference-alignment, but not as a frontier win.
