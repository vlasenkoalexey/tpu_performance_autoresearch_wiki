# Qwen3-8B jax v043b — tp=2 @ bs6 (equal-global-batch control)

## Hypothesis under test

**Hypothesis** (the clean control for [v043](2026-06-05-v043-tp2-s8k.md)): with global batch held at 24 (= v035),
does tp=2's activation sharding lift the frontier above v035 (34.6% / 6,030)? Removes the batch-halving confound
that made v043 (bs3, global 12) uninterpretable.

**Mechanism**: image `qwen3-8b-jax:v033-maxtext-ce` + `--tp_parallelism=2 --batch_size=6`. Mesh `fsdp=4 × tp=2`,
global batch = 6×4 = **24** (matches v035). All four levers ON.

**Predicted signal**: tok/s/chip > 6,030 if tp helps; the a-priori activation-invariance argument predicts ≤.

**Falsification criterion**: ≤ 6,030 beyond noise.

## Setup

- Image `qwen3-8b-jax:v033-maxtext-ce`, `--use_remat --use_splash --use_scan --use_maxtext_ce --batch_size=6

  `global_batch=24 per_chip_batch=6`, `[scan]/[attn]/[remat:nothing_saveable]/[ce]` ON.

## Verdict

**Refuted** — at equal global batch (24), tp=2 is **26% slower in tok/s/chip and 9pp worse MFU** than pure
FSDP. The confound is gone, and tp loses cleanly. This confirms the a-priori argument: at equal global batch
tp trades batch-sharding for model-sharding, leaving the **per-chip activation `[6,8192,6144]` identical to
v035's `[3,8192,12288]`** — so it cannot reduce the SiLU/norm recompute that is the gap — while *adding*
per-layer all-reduce collectives that pure FSDP avoids. **tp is not a path to MaxText's sharded-activation
regime on this stack; MaxText's loop-fusion advantage comes from its host-offload pipelining, not from tp per
se.** seq8192 frontier stays v035 (34.6% / 6,030). **v044 (tp=4) is strictly worse — retired, not run.**
