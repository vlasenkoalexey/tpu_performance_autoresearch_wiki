# Qwen3-8B jax v014 — remat+splash+CE bs=6 (absl-guarded)

## Hypothesis under test

**Hypothesis**: Stacking remat+splash+CE frees HBM for bs=6 (global 48), pushing MFU
past the splash-bs4 frontier (32.4%).

**Mechanism**: `--use_remat --use_splash --use_tokamax_ce --tokamax_ce_impl=mosaic_tpu
--batch_size=6` + vmem flag, image `qwen3-8b-jax:v013-ce`.

## Results

| Metric | v008 (splash bs4) | **v014 (splash+CE bs6)** |
|--------|-------------------|---------------------------|
| Throughput/chip | **6,299** | 5,929 |
| MFU | **32.4%** | **30.5%** |
| Loss | stable ~12.07 | stable 12.10→12.07 (correct) |
| Exit | 0 | 0 |

bs=6 fits (CE+splash clear the memory), loss is sane, but throughput/chip **drops**
(5,929 < 6,299) and MFU falls to 30.5% < 32.4%. The CE streaming overhead + the bs=6
step aren't repaid: **bs=4 is the occupancy sweet spot at seq2048**, and CE adds cost
without a memory constraint to relieve there (bs=4 logits already fit).

## Profile / HLO
- xprof run `2026-06-02-qwen3-jax-v014-splash-ce-bs6` (steps 12–14); HLO in GCS.

## Verdict

**refuted** (as a frontier move) — CE+splash bs=6 = 30.5% MFU, **below** the v008
splash-bs4 frontier (32.4%). CE is confirmed a **memory-enabler, not a throughput
lever** at seq2048 (it lets bs=6 run, but bs=4 without CE is faster per chip). The
seq2048 frontier stays at v008. **Where CE matters**: at seq8192 (v009), where the
`[B,L,V]` logits are larger and bs=1 is the dense ceiling — stacking CE there to
reach seq8192+bs2 is the live follow-up.
