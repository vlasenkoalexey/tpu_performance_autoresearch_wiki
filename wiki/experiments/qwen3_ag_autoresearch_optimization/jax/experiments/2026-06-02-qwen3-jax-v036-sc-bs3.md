# Qwen3-8B jax (Flax NNX) v036: SparseCore Collective Offloads @ bs=3, seq=8192

Testing the offloading of FSDP collectives from TensorCore to SparseCore to see if reclaiming TensorCore cycles improves overall throughput and MFU.

## Hypothesis under test

This experiment tests the [SparseCore collective offloads (jax)](../../../../hypotheses/qwen3-jax-sparsecore-offload.md) hypothesis. Setting `xla_tpu_enable_sparse_core_collective_offload_{all_reduce,reduce_scatter,all_gather}=true` should offload FSDP communication operations, freeing TensorCore cycles for matmul computation and reducing steady step time.

## Baseline comparison

Comparison against the [v035 run](2026-06-02-qwen3-jax-v035-maxtext-ce-s8k-bs3.md) (Fully optimized JAX stack baseline at bs=3, seqlen=8192):

| Run | Offload collectives | Step time | Throughput | tok/s/chip | MFU |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| v035 | None (TensorCore) | 4077.5 ms | 48,218 tok/s | 6,027 | 34.6% |
| **v036** | **All-Reduce, Reduce-Scatter, All-Gather** | **4100.8 ms** | **47,944 tok/s** | **5,993** | **34.4%** |
| **Δ (v036 − v035)** | **Offload all three** | **+0.6%** | **-0.6%** | **-0.6%** | **-0.2 pp** |

Offloading the three collectives to SparseCore results in a minor performance regression (**-0.6%** throughput, **-0.2 pp** MFU).

## Observations

- The cold compilation time for step 0 was **33.5 seconds**, indicating that adding the SparseCore compiler passes didn't significantly increase the JAX compilation duration (compared to `v035`'s 34.7s).
- At batch size 3, JAX's default async collective pipelining and host transfer scheduling are already extremely efficient at overlapping communication with computation. Consequently, moving the collectives to SparseCore fails to reclaim significant TensorCore cycles, and instead introduces additional PCIe/SC transfer and synchronization overheads that slightly outweigh any savings.

## Verdict + reasoning

**refuted** — The hypothesis that offloading all three collectives from TensorCore to SparseCore decreases steady-state step time is refuted, as we observed a steady step time regression from 4077.5 ms to 4100.8 ms (+23.3 ms / +0.6%).
