# Qwen3 JAX current v088 - 2k batch16 VMEM 97280

## Hypothesis under test

**Hypothesis**: v086 improved the 2k batch16 frontier by lowering scoped VMEM
from **100352 KiB** to **98304 KiB**, reaching **70,564 tok/s** and **45.4%**
script MFU. Lowering one more step to **97280 KiB** may continue the same
schedule-quality improvement, or show that **98304 KiB** is the lower-side
frontier.

**Mechanism**: Reuse v086 exactly, changing only
`--xla_tpu_scoped_vmem_limit_kib=97280`. Keep per-chip **batch_size=16**
(`global_batch=128`), `--seqlen=2048`, latency-hiding scheduler rerun **3**,
no-scan remat, activation sharding, Tokamax Splash, MaxText CE, SparseCore
reduce-scatter/all-reduce offload, SparseCore collective aggregator, and
shared-memory limit **90**.

**Predicted signal**: Support requires clean finite loss plus throughput above
v086's **70,564 tok/s**, ideally with XProf step time below **3743.6 ms** or
MXU above **67.4%**. Lower throughput with unchanged memory/profiling refutes
the lower VMEM bracket and keeps **98304 KiB**.

## Verdict

Refuted. Lowering scoped VMEM from **98304 KiB** to **97280 KiB** produced a
distinct train-step HLO but did not improve performance or memory. Best worker
throughput was **70,549 tok/s**, below v086's **70,564 tok/s**; XProf step time
regressed from **3743.6 ms** to **3748.9 ms**; and peak free HBM moved slightly
down from **1.9934 GiB** to **1.9883 GiB**.

Keep **98304 KiB** as the current 2k batch16 VMEM frontier and pivot away from
the local VMEM bracket.
