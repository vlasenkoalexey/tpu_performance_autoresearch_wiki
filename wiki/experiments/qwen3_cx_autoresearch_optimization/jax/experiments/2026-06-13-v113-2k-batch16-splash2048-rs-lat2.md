# Qwen3 JAX current v113 - 2k batch16 Splash BKV2048 reduce-scatter latency multiplier 2

## Hypothesis under test

**Hypothesis**: The frontier has **2,774** reduce-scatter references and uses
SparseCore reduce-scatter offload with
`--xla_tpu_sparse_core_reduce_scatter_latency_multiplier=3`. Lowering the
multiplier to 2 may reduce scheduler over-prioritization of reduce-scatter and
recover a small amount of dense compute scheduling quality, while keeping the
successful offload path enabled.

**Mechanism**: Reuse v095/v096 exactly but change:

```bash
--xla_tpu_sparse_core_reduce_scatter_latency_multiplier=2
```

Keep `--batch_size=16`, `--seqlen=2048`, scoped VMEM **98304 KiB**,
`--xla_tpu_scheduler_percent_shared_memory_limit=100`, no-scan remat,
activation sharding, Tokamax Splash, MaxText CE, SparseCore reduce-scatter and
all-reduce offload, SparseCore collective aggregator, all-gather ACF enabled,
reduce-scatter ACF enabled, all-reduce ACF disabled, multiple-step ACF enabled,
and:

```bash
export SPLASH_BKV=2048
export SPLASH_BKV_COMPUTE=2048
```

**Predicted signal**: Support requires beating the confirmed v095/v096
**70,959-70,976 tok/s** band or matching it with lower XProf step time. A
same-HLO in-band runtime refutes the lower latency multiplier.
