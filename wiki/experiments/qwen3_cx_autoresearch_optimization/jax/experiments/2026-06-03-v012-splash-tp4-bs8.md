# Qwen3 JAX v012 - splash TP4 bs8

## Hypothesis under test

**Hypothesis**: TP=4 plus Splash attention should fit the per-chip batch 8 occupancy point that failed at TP=2, because the vocabulary/head and activation footprint are sharded more aggressively.

**Mechanism**: Run the native Flax NNX trainer with `--use_splash=True`, `--tp_parallelism=4`, and `--batch_size=8` at sequence length 2048. This changes the mesh to `fsdp=2,tp=4`; global batch is 16 on v6e-8.

**Predicted signal**: Clean 20-step run, valid XProf trace, peak HBM below 31.25 GiB, and higher tokens/sec and MFU than the native-JAX baseline.

**Falsification criterion**: Compile-time HBM OOM, no completed profiled window, or throughput/MFU no better than the baseline.

## Verdict

`supported`.

TP=4 plus Splash attention makes the batch-8 occupancy point fit on v6e-8 and moves the native-JAX frontier from **31,955 tok/s / 20.5% MFU** to **39,438 tok/s / 25.4% MFU** at seq_len 2048. The run still leaves substantial headroom: matmuls are only 31.9% of device time and the remaining profile is dominated by loop fusion plus collectives. Peak HBM is high but viable at 27.75 GiB, so this is a valid frontier rather than a marginal OOM-prone configuration.

## Next hypotheses

- Retry streamed/chunked CE at TP=4, because v010/v011 showed the plain CE/log-softmax path as the HBM wall for larger occupancy points.
- Use TP=4 + Splash as the new baseline for seq_len 4096/8192 feasibility checks.
- Inspect whether the all-gather/all-to-all mix can be reduced before increasing sequence length.
