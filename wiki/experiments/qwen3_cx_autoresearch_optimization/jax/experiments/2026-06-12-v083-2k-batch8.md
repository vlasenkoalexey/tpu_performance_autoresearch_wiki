# Qwen3 JAX current v083 - 2k batch8 scale

## Hypothesis under test

**Hypothesis**: v082's per-chip batch **6** improved the 2k frontier to
**67,987 tok/s**, **43.7% script MFU**, and **61.6% MXU**, while still leaving
**9.9688 GiB** runtime HBM free. Increasing per-chip batch to **8** should test
whether short-context throughput is still occupancy-limited or has reached a
memory/schedule scaling wall.

**Mechanism**: Reuse v082 exactly, changing only `--batch_size=8`
(`global_batch=64`) at `--seqlen=2048`. Keep no-scan remat, activation sharding,
Tokamax Splash, MaxText CE, scoped VMEM **100352 KiB**, latency-hiding rerun
**3**, SparseCore reduce-scatter/all-reduce offload, SparseCore collective
aggregator, and shared-memory limit **90**.

**Predicted signal**: Support requires clean finite loss plus throughput above
v082's best-worker **67,987 tok/s** and ideally higher MXU than **61.6%**.
Compile/runtime OOM, strong step-time growth beyond the larger batch, or a
larger custom-call/loop-fusion/data-formatting fraction refutes further direct
batch scaling.

## Verdict

Supported as a marginal tie, not a strong new frontier. Batch scaling from
per-chip **6** to **8** raised best-worker throughput only from v082's
**67,987 tok/s** to **68,184 tok/s** (**+0.3%**) while step-time variance rose
and profile-window throughput dipped to **67.3-68.0k tok/s**. MXU improved from
**61.6%** to **63.2%**, and HBM still has **8.7082 GiB** free, so one larger
batch jump is worth testing; if per-chip **batch_size=12** stays near 68k tok/s,
direct 2k batch scaling should be considered saturated.
