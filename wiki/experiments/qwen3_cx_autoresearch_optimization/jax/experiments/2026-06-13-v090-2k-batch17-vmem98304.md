# Qwen3 JAX current v090 - 2k batch17 VMEM 98304

## Hypothesis under test

**Hypothesis**: v089 batch18 compiled but failed at program load because it
needed **26.25G** bottom-of-memory reservation while **25.52G** was reservable.
Per-chip **batch_size=17** may fit inside that gap and improve throughput over
v086's batch16 **70,564 tok/s** frontier.

**Mechanism**: Reuse v086 exactly, changing only `--batch_size=17`. Keep
`--seqlen=2048`, scoped VMEM **98304 KiB**, latency-hiding scheduler rerun
**3**, no-scan remat, activation sharding, Tokamax Splash, MaxText CE,
SparseCore reduce-scatter/all-reduce offload, SparseCore collective aggregator,
and shared-memory limit **90**.

**Predicted signal**: Support requires clean finite loss plus throughput above
v086's **70,564 tok/s**. Runtime OOM or flat/slower throughput closes direct
batch scaling above batch16.

## Verdict

**Supported tie / provisional frontier.** v090 technically exceeds v086's
**70,564 tok/s** by **6 tok/s**, but that is a **0.009%** delta and should be
treated as run noise until rerun. The batch17 shape also leaves only **1.10 GiB
runtime HBM free**, versus roughly **1.99 GiB** for v086 batch16. Carry v090 only
as best-by-script pending exact rerun; do not probe batch18 further without a
separate memory-reduction mechanism.
