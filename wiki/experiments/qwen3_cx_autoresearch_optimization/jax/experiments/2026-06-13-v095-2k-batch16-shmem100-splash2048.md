# Qwen3 JAX current v095 - 2k batch16 shmem100 Splash BKV2048

## Hypothesis under test

**Hypothesis**: v092/v093 established the current 2k batch16 shmem100 frontier,
but the profile still spends about **13.5%** in Splash/custom-call and the logs
show the default forward Splash tile as `bq=2048 bkv=1024`. At seqlen **2048**,
using a full-sequence forward KV tile may reduce tiling overhead and improve the
custom-call/data-formatting buckets without changing model semantics.

**Mechanism**: Reuse v093 exactly, changing only:

```bash
export SPLASH_BKV=2048
export SPLASH_BKV_COMPUTE=2048
```

Keep `--batch_size=16`, `--seqlen=2048`, scoped VMEM **98304 KiB**,
latency-hiding scheduler rerun **3**, no-scan remat, activation sharding,
Tokamax Splash, MaxText CE, SparseCore reduce-scatter/all-reduce offload,
SparseCore collective aggregator, and
`--xla_tpu_scheduler_percent_shared_memory_limit=100`.

**Predicted signal**: Support requires clean finite loss plus throughput above
v092's **70,606 tok/s**, or a same-band run with materially lower
custom-call/data-formatting time and no HBM regression. Slower throughput,
higher custom-call time, or an OOM refutes carrying the larger forward KV tile.

## Verdict

**Supported / provisional new 2k frontier.** v095 beats v092's previous
high-water mark (**70,606 tok/s**) by about **370 tok/s** and improves XProf
step time from v093's **3741.4 ms** to **3720.2 ms**. The profile shows the
intended attention-tile mechanism: custom-call time drops by about **438 ms**
over the v093 profile, and the optimized HLO is materially smaller with fewer
copy/fusion/Splash strings.

Carry `SPLASH_BKV=2048 SPLASH_BKV_COMPUTE=2048` only provisionally because HBM
margin is extremely tight (**0.10 GiB free**) and the gain needs an exact rerun.
The next experiment should rerun v095 unchanged with a fresh cache/profile.
