# Qwen3 JAX current v087 - 2k batch16 scheduler rerun4

## Hypothesis under test

**Hypothesis**: v086 improved the 2k batch16 frontier to **70,564 tok/s** and
**45.4% script MFU** by changing scoped VMEM to **98304 KiB**, while runtime
HBM remained near the same ceiling as v085. Since the gain appears to be
schedule quality rather than memory relief, increasing
`--xla_latency_hiding_scheduler_rerun` from **3** to **4** may find a better
schedule at the same shape.

**Mechanism**: Reuse v086 exactly, changing only
`--xla_latency_hiding_scheduler_rerun=4`. Keep per-chip **batch_size=16**
(`global_batch=128`), `--seqlen=2048`, scoped VMEM **98304 KiB**, no-scan remat,
activation sharding, Tokamax Splash, MaxText CE, SparseCore reduce-scatter and
all-reduce offload, SparseCore collective aggregator, and shared-memory limit
**90**.

**Predicted signal**: Support requires clean finite loss plus throughput above
v086's **70,564 tok/s**, or matching throughput with meaningfully lower XProf
step-time variance. Lower throughput or identical HLO/performance refutes
rerun4 at this 2k frontier.

## Verdict

Refuted/tie. The train-step HLO is byte-identical to v086 and runtime memory is
unchanged, but performance is slightly worse: best worker **70,554 tok/s** vs
v086's **70,564 tok/s**, XProf step time **3746.5 ms** vs **3743.6 ms**, stddev
**3.3 ms** vs **2.5 ms**, and MXU **67.2%** vs **67.4%**.

Do not carry `--xla_latency_hiding_scheduler_rerun=4`; keep v086's rerun3 /
VMEM98304 as the current 2k batch16 frontier.
