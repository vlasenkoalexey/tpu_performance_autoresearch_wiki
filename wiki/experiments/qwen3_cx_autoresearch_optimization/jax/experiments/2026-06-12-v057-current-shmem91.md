# Qwen3 JAX current v057 - shared-memory limit 91

## Hypothesis under test

**Hypothesis**: v056 showed 92% selects a tight old-v273-style schedule with
only 0.318 GiB free HBM and no speedup. The transition from the supported 90%
schedule to the tight high-side schedule may occur between 90 and 92. Testing
91 isolates that threshold.

**Mechanism**: Keep the v037 stack fixed and change only
`--xla_tpu_scheduler_percent_shared_memory_limit` from `90` to `91`.

**Predicted signal**: Support requires clean finite loss and throughput above
the v036/v037 frontier (**59,330 tok/s / 4433.0 ms**), or at least preserving
the low-HBM-pressure 90% schedule with a same-band speedup.

**Falsification criterion**: Compile/runtime failure, invalid loss, HBM
pressure comparable to v056/v039, or clean completion below v036/v037.

## Result

v036/v037 frontier. Worker0 reported **58,947 tok/s / 7,368 tok/s/chip /
42.3% old MFU** and worker1 reported **58,970 tok/s / 7,371 tok/s/chip /
42.3% old MFU**.

## Verdict

Refuted. The 91% shared-memory setting falls into the same byte-identical
high-side schedule as v056 rather than improving the supported 90% point.
Throughput remains below v036/v037 (**59,330 tok/s / 4433.0 ms**), so keep
`--xla_tpu_scheduler_percent_shared_memory_limit=90` and close this
high-side shared-memory axis.
