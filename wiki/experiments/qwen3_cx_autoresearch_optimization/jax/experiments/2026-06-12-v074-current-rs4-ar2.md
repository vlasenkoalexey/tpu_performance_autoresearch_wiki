# Qwen3 JAX current v074 - explicit-SiLU stack with RS4+AR2

## Hypothesis under test

**Hypothesis**: The older current-cluster RS4+AR2 probe was run on the v030/v273
family with shared-memory limit 100 and the larger pre-v037/pre-explicit-SiLU
HLO. The current carried family is the smaller v067/v068 explicit-SiLU shmem90
HLO with lower HBM and fewer collectives/fusions. Raising only the
reduce-scatter SparseCore latency multiplier from 3 to 4 may select a better
schedule on this current HLO even though it regressed the older family.

**Mechanism**: Rerun the v067/v068 explicit-SiLU image on np0 with a fresh
compile cache and change only:

```text
--xla_tpu_sparse_core_reduce_scatter_latency_multiplier=4
```

Everything else stays fixed: no-scan/remat, activation sharding, MaxText CE,
Tokamax Splash max-logit 30, batch size 4 per chip, `fsdp=8,tp=1`,
all-gather on async collective fusion, SparseCore reduce-scatter/all-reduce
offload with aggregator, AR latency multiplier 2, scoped VMEM 100352,
shared-memory limit 90, scheduler rerun 3, normal offload tracing, and np0
placement.

**Predicted signal**: Support requires clean finite loss and either throughput
above the current durable **59.33k tok/s / 4433.8 ms** band or a lower XProf
step time/profile breakdown with unchanged HBM. HBM regression, changed HLO
with slower profile, or same/lower throughput refutes the probe.

## Verdict

Refuted. RS4+AR2 selects a distinct current-HLO schedule, but it increases HBM
by about **0.23 GiB**, lowers host throughput, and regresses XProf step time
versus the RS3+AR2 v068/v071 band. Keep reduce-scatter SparseCore latency
multiplier `3`.
