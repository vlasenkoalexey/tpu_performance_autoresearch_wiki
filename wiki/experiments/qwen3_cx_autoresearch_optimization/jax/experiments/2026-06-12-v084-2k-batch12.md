# Qwen3 JAX current v084 - 2k batch12 scale

## Hypothesis under test

**Hypothesis**: v083 improved script MFU only marginally to **43.8%** but still
left **8.7082 GiB** runtime HBM free and reached **63.2% MXU**. A larger jump to
per-chip **batch_size=12** (`global_batch=96`) should either improve throughput
by amortizing fixed short-context overhead further, or clearly show that direct
batch scaling has saturated.

**Mechanism**: Reuse v083 exactly, changing only `--batch_size=12`
(`global_batch=96`) at `--seqlen=2048`. Keep no-scan remat, activation sharding,
Tokamax Splash, MaxText CE, scoped VMEM **100352 KiB**, latency-hiding rerun
**3**, SparseCore reduce-scatter/all-reduce offload, SparseCore collective
aggregator, and shared-memory limit **90**.

**Predicted signal**: Support requires clean finite loss plus throughput
meaningfully above v083's **68,184 tok/s**. A result near **68k tok/s**, OOM, or
larger custom-call/loop-fusion/data-formatting share refutes further direct
batch scaling and should pivot the 2k lane away from batch size.

## Verdict

Supported. Batch scaling from per-chip **8** to **12** improved best-worker
throughput from v083's **68,184 tok/s** to **69,547 tok/s** (**+2.0%**) and
raised MXU from **63.2%** to **65.5%** with low profile-step variance. Runtime
HBM still leaves **5.0216 GiB** free, so one near-ceiling batch-size probe at
per-chip **batch_size=16** is justified before pivoting away from direct batch
scaling.
