# Qwen3 JAX current v104 - 2k batch16 Splash BKV2048 shmem93

## Hypothesis under test

**Hypothesis**: The BKV2048 frontier has a sharp shared-memory threshold.
shmem95 preserved speed but remained HBM-tight, while shmem92 and shmem90 both
dropped peak HBM to **29.24 GiB** with about **2.002 GiB** free but regressed
script throughput below the v095/v096 frontier. A one-step midpoint at
**shmem93** may hit the headroom transition with less schedule damage.

**Mechanism**: Reuse v095/v096 exactly and change only
`--xla_tpu_scheduler_percent_shared_memory_limit` to **93**. Keep
`--batch_size=16`, `--seqlen=2048`, scoped VMEM **98304 KiB**, no-scan remat,
activation sharding, Tokamax Splash, MaxText CE, SparseCore reduce-scatter and
all-reduce offload, SparseCore collective aggregator, and:

```bash
export SPLASH_BKV=2048
export SPLASH_BKV_COMPUTE=2048
```

**Predicted signal**: Support requires either beating the confirmed v095/v096
speed band (**70.95k tok/s**) or matching it while gaining material HBM
headroom. If shmem93 compiles to the v101/v103 HLO and stays around
70.93k tok/s, it closes the shmem bracket but does not become the speed
frontier.

## Verdict

**Refuted for speed and headroom.** shmem93 is slightly faster than shmem92 but
still below the confirmed v095/v096 frontier, and it remains on the tight-HBM
side of the threshold (**0.1018 GiB** free). The transition is between shmem92
and shmem93: shmem92/90 give ~2.0 GiB free but slower runtime, while
shmem93/95/100 are HBM-tight and faster. Do not spend another run on shmem94
unless the goal changes to noise characterization; pivot to another scheduler
axis on the v095/v096 BKV2048 frontier.
